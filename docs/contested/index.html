⚠ Negative Finding Cohort 2026 Gut Microbiome

Plant-based diets, gut microbiota, blood metabolome, and risk of colorectal, liver, and pancreatic cancers: results from a large prospective cohort study of predominantly low-income Americans.

Yuan F, Wang L, Nguyen SM et al. — Am J Clin Nutr , 2026 — PMID 41391639

WHAT THIS STUDY CLAIMS

This study found that plant-based diets were associated with a higher risk of certain cancers, including colorectal, liver, and pancreatic cancers, compared to non-plant-based diets.

LIMITATIONS

Observational design, self-reported dietary data, healthy user bias, limited generalisability

WHAT THE BROADER EVIDENCE SAYS

The finding of increased cancer risk with plant-based diets is contradicted by studies that show plant-based diets can improve gut microbiota and reduce the risk of certain cancers. For example, PMID: 38260058 found that dietary intervention, including plant-based diets, can improve metabolic levels in patients with type 2 diabetes through the gut microbiota.

Contradicted by:

  • Dietary intervention improves metabolic levels in patients with type 2 diabetes through the gut microbiota: a systematic review and meta-analysis.
⚠ Negative Finding Cohort 2026 Gut Microbiome

Potato Protein-Based Vegan Burgers: Discovering the Health-Promoting Benefits and Impact on the Intestinal Microbiome.

Kowalczewski PŁ, Gumienna M, Jeżowski P et al. — Nutrients , 2026 — PMID 41515276

WHAT THIS STUDY CLAIMS

This study found that potato protein-based vegan burgers had a limited impact on the intestinal microbiome.

LIMITATIONS

Small sample size, short follow-up, surrogate markers, limited generalisability

WHAT THE BROADER EVIDENCE SAYS

The finding of limited impact on the intestinal microbiome is contradicted by studies that show plant-based diets can have a positive effect on the gut microbiota. For example, PMID: 36986240 found that plant-based diets can improve the gut microbiota in interventional studies.

Contradicted by:

  • Effect of Plant-Based Diets on Gut Microbiota: A Systematic Review of Interventional Studies.
⚠ Negative Finding Cohort 2026 Protein Adequacy & Muscle Mass

Development of the Vegan Protein Quality (VPQ) tool to derive smarter vegan meals with high protein quality.

Soh BXP, Vignes M, Smith NW et al. — Sci Rep , 2026 — PMID 42000958

WHAT THIS STUDY CLAIMS

This study found that plant foods generally supply lower quantities of digestible indispensable amino acids relative to the metabolic requirements, which may be detrimental to muscle mass.

LIMITATIONS

Observational study, self-reported dietary data, limited generalisability, no control group

WHAT THE BROADER EVIDENCE SAYS

The finding that plant-based diets may be lower in indispensable amino acids is contradicted by a meta-analysis (PMID: 41994089) which found that plant-based protein supplementation had similar effects on body composition and muscle strength as animal-based protein supplementation.

Contradicted by:

  • Long-term effects of plant vs. animal protein supplementation on body composition, muscle strength, physical performance, and cardiometabolic risk factors in adults:a systematic review and meta-analysis of randomized controlled trials.
