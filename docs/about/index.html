About This Project

Methodology

This site automatically retrieves peer-reviewed studies from PubMed using the NCBI E-utilities API. Searches are run daily via GitHub Actions. Studies are classified by study type and quality tier using PubMed publication type metadata.

Narrative summaries are generated by a large language model (Llama 3.3 70B via Groq) instructed to cite sources, use direct quotes, and distinguish evidence quality. Summaries are regenerated when new studies are added.

This site does not provide medical advice. All content is for informational purposes. Consult a qualified healthcare professional before making dietary changes.

Evidence Hierarchy

Tier Study Type Description Confidence
1 Meta-Analysis / Systematic Review Pooled analysis of multiple studies with rigorous inclusion criteria. Highest evidence quality. Highest
2 Randomized Controlled Trial (RCT) Participants randomly assigned to intervention or control. Can establish causation. High
3 Cohort / Observational Study Follows groups over time, observing outcomes. Can establish association, not causation. Moderate
4 Cross-Sectional Study Snapshot data at a single point in time. Can show association only. Low–Moderate
5 Case Report / Editorial / Opinion Single case or expert opinion. Not generalizable. Low

Glossary

Healthy User Bias
A confounding factor where people who adopt a particular diet (e.g., plant-based) are also more likely to engage in other health-promoting behaviors (exercise, not smoking), making it difficult to isolate the dietary effect.
Surrogate Marker
A measurable biological indicator (e.g., LDL cholesterol, BMI, blood glucose) used as a substitute for a clinically meaningful endpoint (e.g., heart attack, death). Changes in surrogate markers do not always predict changes in hard endpoints.
Confounding Variable
A variable that is correlated with both the exposure (diet) and the outcome (health metric), potentially distorting the apparent relationship between them.
Self-Reported Data
Dietary information collected via questionnaires or food frequency questionnaires (FFQs) completed by participants. Subject to recall bias and social desirability bias.
Meta-Analysis
A statistical technique that combines results from multiple independent studies on the same question to produce a pooled estimate with greater statistical power.
Randomized Controlled Trial (RCT)
An experimental study where participants are randomly assigned to an intervention group or a control group, allowing researchers to infer causation.
Cohort Study
An observational study that follows a group of people over time, comparing those exposed to a factor (e.g., a plant-based diet) with those not exposed, to see who develops a given outcome.
Cross-Sectional Study
A study that examines a population at a single point in time, measuring both exposure and outcome simultaneously. Cannot establish temporal sequence or causation.

Update Frequency

The database is updated every day at 06:00 UTC via a GitHub Actions workflow. New studies published in the past year are fetched on each run. Summaries are regenerated for topics that have received new studies. All summaries are regenerated every Sunday.

You can also trigger a manual update from the GitHub repository's Actions tab.
